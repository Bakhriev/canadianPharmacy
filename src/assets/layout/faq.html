The Present and Future of the Canadian Pharmaceutical Industry

The Canadian pharmaceutical industry has come a long way, evolving from a small number of local apothecaries compounding basic medicines for the needs of their communities into one of the global healthcare leaders and standard-setters for reliability and excellence. Ranking ninth on the list of major medicine producers, Canada supplies its pharmaceuticals to more than 150 countries worldwide.

Here are some curious stats on the Canadian pharmaceutical industry:

  • Canada has 12,000+ registered pharmacies on its territory and 48,000+ licensed pharmacists
  • In 2022, Canada’s pharmaceutical market was worth about $83.08 billion, and it is expected to grow to about $141.69 billion by 2030
  • The top five drug manufacturers in the country are Johnson and Johnson, Teva Canada, Sanofi, Pfizer, and Novartis
  • Generic drugs have gained a significant market share in Canada over the past two decades, with 74.4 % of all prescriptions filled in 2022 being for generics

Competing against cheaper and much less strictly regulated pharmaceutical markets of China, Brazil, and India, Canada is trying to maintain its leading position by actively investing in scientific research and innovation development. Apart from promoting domestic public-private-academic collaborations, Canada encourages international partnerships to allow smaller companies to tap into global expertise.
